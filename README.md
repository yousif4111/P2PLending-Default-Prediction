# 🔍 P2PLending-Default-Prediction
Peer-to-Peer Lending or P2P Lending refers to a type of crowdfunding where people loan money to one another. 💸🤝
Borrowers issue legally binding loans to investors in return for providing capital. 💼🏦
This model removes banks and financial institutions, reducing fees and increasing profitability. 💰💹

## 🔬 Methodology

The study proposes a methodology to predict default in P2P lending by analyzing data from Capsphere, a prominent P2P lending platform. 📊🔍
The goal is to determine factors causing default in P2P online lending and develop predictive models using machine learning algorithms. 🎯🤖

The research identifies features that play a role in predicting default in P2P online lending, such as employment length, loan interest rate, and loan term. ⏳💰
Four predictive models, including Logistics Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting, are evaluated for their performance in predicting default. 📈🌲🌪️⚡

## Findings

Credit score, debt-to-income ratio, loan amount, and interest rate are most important factors for determining default in many online P2P lending platforms.
